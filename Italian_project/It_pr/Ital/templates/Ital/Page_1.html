We will now explain in detail what choices you have to make in the first part of the experiment. The first part consists of 10 periods.
At the end of the experiment, if Part 1 is selected for the final payout, we will draw one of the ten periods randomly with equal probability. Your final earnings will be given by the earnings you obtained in the drawn period plus 3 euros for submitting to the experiment.

At the beginning of the first part, you will be randomly and anonymously grouped in groups of 11 members. The composition of the group will remain unchanged for all 10 periods of this first part. None of you will be able to know the identity of the other members of your group or of other groups either during the experiment or afterwards.

Within each group of 11 members, there will be the ALPHA party, consisting of 4 members and a "candidate A" and the BETA party, consisting of 5 members and a "candidate B".
The role of ALPHA party member and candidate A, as well as the role of BETA party member and candidate B, are assigned in a completely random manner and will remain unchanged for all 10 periods of the first part.

Each of you will then be informed whether you are a member of the ALPHA party or a member of the BETA party or whether you have been assigned the role of candidate A or candidate B.

Each member of the ALPHA party and the BETA party will be awarded a Y bonus. We will see what this means in a moment.
Next, each ALPHA and BETA party member will be asked to choose one of two options: the "VOTE" option or the "ABSTAIN" option. To make this choice you simply have to select one of the two options and press the "OK" button that you will see on the screen.

However, before the ALPHA and BETA party members decide whether to vote or abstain, candidate A and candidate B have the option of subtracting a vote from the other party and adding it to their own party. The cost of this operation is the same for both candidate A and candidate B, it varies from period to period and can be 20 points or 40 points, and in each period all participants will be informed of its amount.

Let us take an example:

Suppose candidate A decided to subtract one vote from the BETA party and add it to the ALPHA party while candidate B did not use this tool.

- If the BETA party has at least one individual who has chosen to vote then this vote will automatically be added to the ALPHA party and subtracted from the BETA party.

- If in the BETA party no individual has chosen to vote then a vote will automatically be added to the ALPHA party while there will be no change for the BETA party. 

Suppose both candidate A and candidate B have decided to subtract a vote from the other party, in this case there will be no change in the vote count for the two parties.

After both candidate A and candidate B have chosen whether or not to subtract a vote from the BETA and ALPHA parties respectively and have been informed of this by each other, candidate A and candidate B will be able to choose which of the following messages to send to both the ALPHA and BETA party members.

Messages to choose from for candidate A:

- Candidate B has chosen to subtract one vote from the ALPHA party.

- Candidate B has chosen not to deduct any votes from the ALPHA party

- No communication

Messages to choose from for candidate B:

- Candidate A has chosen to subtract one vote from the BETA party

- Candidate A has chosen not to deduct any votes from the BETA party

- No communication

Finally, after receiving the messages from candidate A and candidate B, the ALPHA and BETA party members will proceed to choose whether to vote or abstain.

The ALPHA and BETA party members will not be informed of the actual choices made by the candidates regarding vote abstention, neither during nor at the end of the experiment.

After each ALPHA and BETA party member has chosen to vote or abstain, the gain for that period for both candidate A and B and the ALPHA and BETA party members will be shown to you on the screen.
