Let us take an example:

Suppose that the cost of subtracting a mark is 20 points. Suppose further that candidate A decides to proceed with vote subtraction while candidate B decides not to use this tool.

Finally, suppose that one ALPHA party member and two BETA party members choose to vote.

To calculate each participant's payoff we need to take into account that candidate A chooses to subtract one vote and that therefore the ALPHA party has more votes than the BETA party.

The participants' payoffs will thus be calculated for this period:

- the ALPHA party member who chose to vote will receive 5 points. The other four ALPHA members who chose to abstain will receive 5 points plus their personal Y bonus.

- The two BETA party members who chose to vote will receive 105 points while the three other BETA party members who chose to abstain will receive 105 points plus their personal Y bonus.

- Finally, candidate A will receive 210-40=170 points while candidate B will receive 60 points.

In each period you will be able to see whether it is the ALPHA party or the BETA party that has the most votes in a table that will automatically update at the end of each period. At the end of each period you will also be informed about your earnings in that period and your earnings in previous periods.
